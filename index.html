Based on our dataset, there is a total of 7500 languages worldwide. However, many of these languages are on the verge of collapse and extinction. Languages are split into different categories based on their frequency of usage. Here are the categorizations: 1. Institutional: A developed language that is widely used by communities and institutions to exchange ideas across a large population. 2. Developing: Languages that are in standardized forms and used by some. However, these languages are not yet widespread or sustainable. 3. Vigorous: Languages that are not developing, but they still enjoy vigorous face-to-face use in daily life by all generations. 4. In trouble: language is used by all generations, but not transmitted to new generations. 5. Dying: Only the grandparents in the family are using the language, and newer generation is not learning the language. 6. Extinct: The language is no longer used by anyone.
